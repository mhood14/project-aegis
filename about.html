About

Why Project Aegis exists and what I wanted to learn from it.

Project Aegis started as a way to learn by building: take a secure-AI idea seriously, make the tradeoffs visible, and document the parts that usually get hand-waved away.

01

Starting point

I wanted to build something that felt closer to a real secure platform than a one-page AI demo. Project Aegis became the place to explore that seriously, without pretending the hard parts would solve themselves.

02

What I was trying to learn

I wanted hands-on experience with Azure architecture, identity controls, network segmentation, managed identity, private service access, retrieval design, hybrid search, and detection-oriented logging.

03

What success looked like

The goal was not just to make the app answer questions. It was to make the whole system more understandable: who can access what, where answers come from, how logs tell the story afterward, and how the design reacts under pressure.

04

Why the site is structured this way

The home page stays lighter and easier to scan, while the deeper pages break the project into Azure setup, networking, application behavior, and logging/testing. That reflects the actual skills involved instead of flattening everything into one dense wall of copy.
